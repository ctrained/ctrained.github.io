Winter Wrap Up

Running streak, yoga, raw vegan

  • March 10th, 2014
  • Raw, Running, Vegan
  • Lisette Oropesa

This winter I ran more miles than I ever have in one season, thanks to my ongoing streak. I challenged myself to run daily (minimum one mile) beginning on Thanksgiving Day, and continuing on till New Year’s Day. But then I didn’t stop there! I enjoyed the daily habit so much I decided to extend it to Valentine’s Day, which came and went and here I am still running every single day. It has been such an enjoyable challenge and adventure. The weather has been less than reasonable; so, much of the time I was on the treadmill in my gym, but the purpose was still very strong in my heart.

In yoga class we learn to set an intention for our practice at the beginning of the session, as it helps to keep the focus singular and present. It’s a great thing to do for any goal: set an intention, a purpose. When I set the goal of running daily it was with the intention that I’d improve in my speed and mileage, and that is exactly what happened. One of the fears I had before starting the streak was that I wouldn’t be able to recover fast enough for a daily run, and that I’d get injured. In fact I feel stronger and am recovering very quickly. Running daily is great training for listening to your body. I’m running by feel, pushing with confidence through discomfort, and holding back with confidence when there’s a signal to do so. It has taught me so very much about what my body is capable of, which is much more than I comfortably give it credit for!

The other thing I’ve been practicing for a year now is eating more raw vegan foods. I got a dehydrator and a juicer last summer and I’ve been using them regularly. The treats coming out of my kitchen are not only very healthy and fresh, but they represent a return to the absolute basics for me. It is so important to me in all aspects of my life not to get carried away by the fluff and the superfluous, but to always remember the core source...whatever that is to me in any situation. In both singing and running it is the breath. That is the great energy. Food that is made ripe by the sun is the most perfect, and when I eat this way, I feel completely in touch with the earth and our source of light and life in the deepest manner. It is really a spiritual experience that I treasure.

As this winter comes to a close and spring emerges, I am setting an intention to continue on the path of listening to my body and feeding it the best way that I know how. I want to let go of the unnecessary and hold fast to that which really matters.
